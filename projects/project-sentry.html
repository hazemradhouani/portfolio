PRO · Small Scale

Sentry

Location Morocco
Year 2024
Studio Yassir Khalil Studio
Type Gatehouse Pavilion
Surface 38 m²

A gatehouse pavilion for a private estate in Morocco, conceived as a compressed architectural object that must perform multiple functions within a very small footprint: security control, covered waiting, and a visual landmark that announces arrival. The building is positioned at the boundary between the public road and the private domain, and its geometry is derived from this threshold condition.

The structure is masonry with a folded concrete roof, the form of which creates a sheltered zone without enclosure, open to the landscape on three sides. The pavilion is detailed to require minimal maintenance and to age gracefully in the Moroccan climate, its material palette of raw concrete, local stone, and oxidised steel speaking to the site's rural and agricultural character.
